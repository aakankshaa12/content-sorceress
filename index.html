Interview Guides

Exponent

Senior Technical Contributor · December 2024–Present

The Brief

Exponent needed interview guides that could rank organically and convert free users to paid course subscribers. These guides are the platform's highest-ROI content and the primary entry point into the funnel.

My Role

I have written 35 guides across PM, TPM, EM, Designer, Growth Analyst, and Strategy/BizOps roles. 25 were written entirely from scratch, and 10 involved research, fact-checking, and editing AI-assisted drafts into publish-ready content.

Each guide is built on two layers of research. For primary research, I talk to subject matter experts who have interviewed at or hired for the specific company and role. For secondary research, I dig through Glassdoor, Blind, Quora, Reddit, Medium, LinkedIn, and YouTube to surface specific candidate accounts, interviewer patterns, and round-by-round details that are otherwise scattered across the internet.

What Makes These Hard to Write

A good interview guide knows how Google's PM interview differs from Meta's, what a DoorDash strategy round looks for versus a Stripe one, and what candidates consistently get wrong at each company. That level of specificity comes from doing the research at depth and having enough domain fluency to know what is signal and what is noise.

Results

Exponent's interview guides are its primary free-to-paid conversion driver. My 35 guides are a significant part of that content library, with the Google PM, Meta PM, and Facebook EM guides ranking among the platform's top-converting pages.

Newsletter Writer · August 2024–May 2025

The Brief

CapitalWe is a financial planning firm serving Indian immigrants in the US: H1B, L1, and Green Card holders navigating an unfamiliar financial system. Vrishin, the founder, needed a fortnightly newsletter that could educate his audience, build trust, and convert readers into consultation clients.

My Role

I ghostwrote 25+ fortnightly newsletters entirely in Vrishin's voice, covering personal finance, investing, tax planning, and immigration-specific money decisions. The newsletter functions as TOFU content, warming readers up for a free consultation and eventual client conversion.

My Process

Every newsletter started with a seed idea from Vrishin: sometimes a question from a client, sometimes a news event, sometimes a financial concept worth demystifying. My job was to turn that seed into a piece that felt personal, grounded in data, and genuinely useful for someone figuring out the US financial system for the first time.

Writing in someone else's voice meant getting Vrishin's tone right: warm, direct, and never condescending about money. Each issue was researched, written, and delivered to a fortnightly deadline without exception.

Content Lead · SaaS / LMS

Thriving Springs

Content Lead · November–December 2024

The Brief

Thriving Springs is a pre-seed AI-powered LMS startup that needed to build its blog engine from scratch. They had no existing content operation: no team, no briefs, no process. The goal was to establish organic content as a growth channel from zero. The engagement ran for two months, limited by the startup's early-stage funding.

My Role

I came in as the full content lead, not just a writer or editor, but the person responsible for standing up the entire operation. I sourced the client through my network, assembled a two-writer team also from my network, and managed the end-to-end content pipeline from brief to final delivery.

My Process

  • Client relationship

    Managed the relationship directly with their marketing manager, handling scope, timelines, feedback cycles, and final delivery.

  • Team building

    Sourced and onboarded two freelance writers from my network, matching them to LMS and e-learning topics that suited their strengths.

  • Brief writing

    Every blog started with a brief I drafted, covering the target keyword, intended audience, content structure, key angles, and internal linking opportunities.

  • Editing and feedback

    Reviewed every draft, gave detailed written feedback, and edited final versions into publish-ready content before delivery.

Results

In two months, Thriving Springs went from no content operation to a functioning blog engine with a library of published LMS and e-learning guides targeting high-intent B2B keywords.

Research Papers · Consulting

IMA India

Research Writer · 20 papers over 6 months

The Brief

IMA India is a boutique consulting firm whose monthly research papers go out to 1,000+ CXOs on a subscription basis. The papers keep IMA top of mind for its members: CMOs, CHROs, and other senior leaders, and carry real value as executive-level thought leadership.

My Role

I researched and wrote consulting papers based on roundtable discussions with senior business leaders. Each paper synthesized the key insights, debates, and takeaways from those conversations into a polished, publication-ready report for a CXO audience. Over six months, I wrote 20 papers, roughly one every nine days, covering topics across marketing, HR, strategy, and leadership.

What Makes These Hard to Write

CXO readers are time-poor and skeptical. A paper that reads like a summary gets skipped. One that reads like genuine analysis gets shared. That meant understanding the roundtable material deeply enough to have a perspective on it, not just report it.
