Interview Guides

Exponent

Senior Technical Contributor · December 2024–Present

The Brief

Exponent needed interview guides that could rank organically and convert free users to paid course subscribers. These guides are the platform's highest-ROI content and the primary entry point into the funnel.

My Role

I have written 35 guides across PM, TPM, EM, Designer, Growth Analyst, and Strategy/BizOps roles. 25 were written entirely from scratch, and 10 involved research, fact-checking, and editing AI-assisted drafts into publish-ready content.

Each guide is built on two layers of research. For primary research, I talk to subject matter experts who have interviewed at or hired for the specific company and role. For secondary research, I dig through Glassdoor, Blind, Quora, Reddit, Medium, LinkedIn, and YouTube to surface specific candidate accounts, interviewer patterns, and round-by-round details that are otherwise scattered across the internet.

What Makes These Hard to Write

A good interview guide knows how Google's PM interview differs from Meta's, what a DoorDash strategy round looks for versus a Stripe one, and what candidates consistently get wrong at each company. That level of specificity comes from doing the research at depth and having enough domain fluency to know what is signal and what is noise.

Results

Exponent's interview guides are its primary free-to-paid conversion driver. My 35 guides are a significant part of that content library, with the Google PM, Meta PM, and Facebook EM guides ranking among the platform's top-converting pages.

Startups Journalist

HT Mint

Full-time Journalist · 2016–2018

The Brief

Mint's startup and technology desk covered India's rapidly evolving venture ecosystem from early-stage funding and business strategy to layoffs, regulation, and digital consumer trends. The role required balancing speed with depth: breaking news quickly while building enough trust within the ecosystem to report nuanced, insider-driven stories.

My Role

I extensively reported on India's startup ecosystem, covering fintech, edtech, e-commerce, SaaS, and consumer internet businesses. Over the course of my tenure, I wrote more than 150 stories by cultivating sources across organizational hierarchies from founders and board members to operational teams and employees on the ground.

My Process

My reporting involved sourcing, interviewing, fact-checking, and writing both daily news coverage and longer-form enterprise stories. I regularly interviewed founders, operators, investors, and analysts to uncover business shifts, funding developments, and operational realities behind headline growth numbers.

I interviewed industry leaders including Amazon India VP Manish Tiwary, Uber India's Pradeep Parameswaran, Grofers founder Albinder Dhindsa, alongside venture capital investors, analysts, and startup executives to report exclusive and high-impact stories.

Startup journalism is often a race for information, but the best reporting comes from context and relationships. My reporting process relied heavily on source-building across the ecosystem not just leadership teams, but former employees, vendors, competitors, consultants, and junior operators who often understood the business reality more intimately than official statements did.

For breaking news, I worked quickly to validate information across multiple independent sources before publication. For long-form features, I combined interviews with market research, company filings, industry reports, and trend analysis to build stories that explained broader shifts in India's startup ecosystem.

Results

My work contributed to Mint's startup and technology coverage during a period of rapid growth in India's venture-backed economy. I consistently broke funding, leadership, and operational stories while also producing long-form features and analytical reportage on emerging startup trends.
